On Feminism and the Role of Women

A response to “Bad Feminist” by Roxane Gay and “Why we have too few women leaders” by Sheryl Sandberg.

﻿

﻿

These articles make it seem like there is, in fact, a right way to be a woman and a wrong way to be a woman. And the standard appears to be ever changing and unachievable - excerpt from "Bad Feminist" by Roxane Gay

﻿

The essay “Bad Feminist” by Roxane Gay, a writer of Haitian descent, talks about what a “socially acceptable feminist” should be like. The Ted Talk “Why we have too few women leaders” presented by Sheryl Sandberg, an American billionaire and COO of Facebook, touches upon the differences in self-worth in the workforce between women and men as well as the impact the thought of having a child makes on a woman's employment. Although both had similar opinions of how society shapes women, their creators’ variances in life experience invoked a different tone and details included in their works.

﻿

A point that both Roxane and Sheryl brought up was that women who express their thoughts and ask for what they want won’t be liked as much as women who stay quiet and do what they were assigned to do. However, the converse is true for men. Sheryl explains it using a statistic, “success and likeability are positively correlated for men and negative for women” as well as a case study in which changing the gender of an outgoing, successful person decreased their likability. Roxane describes it with a personal story where her ex-boyfriend expounded to her about how women “should” talk to men and called her a feminist (like it was a bad thing) when she disagreed with him.

﻿

Their point on likability was really interesting to me because if society doesn’t like you doing something, you will be motivated to change for a greater sense of belonging and acceptance. I never really thought about it like that before. This also correlates with self-esteem because the things that build self-esteem (ex. experiencing being in important positions, asking and then getting what you want, speaking out and having people agree with you) are also what women are not encouraged to do.
