Soccer All-Star Christoph Clean’s Kick Unearths Diamond

By: Amy Li

On 9 June 2021, the last day of the World Cup finals, Canada played Djibouti in Amsterdam’s ArenA soccer stadium. During the third match, Christoph Clean, an award-winning Canadian playing offense, kicked the soccer ball towards the sky at an estimated 90km/h. Upon landing, the ball formed a crater in the stadium floor, exposing a 100-carat diamond.

﻿

In a previous interview with Forbes, Clean stated that he had been practicing his vertical kick ever since he was three years old. The kick has the power to send the ball flying up 222m above ground. Clean reported that he knocked down multiple birds in his lifetime. 

﻿

When asked why he uses this technique in games, he replied, “my kick has the ability to lift our opponents’ heads towards the sky, buying seconds for our team to reassemble and score the goal. It never gets old really.” 50% of all goals Canada scored during games with Clean has been seconds after his notorious kick.

﻿

Clean’s kick also helped him score during the World Cup finals, but instead of landing a goal, the ball’s landing formed a crater in the ground, revealing a shiny metal. Upon closer inspection, it was identified to be a never seen before, 100-carat diamond. All proceedings of the match halted due to the shocking discovery.

﻿

The event was brought to the attention of the stadium owner, Bruhmo Mint, who claimed that he was the owner of the diamond. “This is private property owned by me,” said Mint, “the legal paperwork states that everything inside this stadium belongs to myself and myself only. That unlawful Mr. Clean ain’t getting nothing.”

Christoph Clean (right) performing his kick as the soccer ball lands next to Tony Robbins (left). 

﻿

Clean responded with, “Come on man, no one would have exposed the loot except for me. Without me bringing spice to every game, you would have sold this stadium long ago. Besides, I’m proposing to my girl in a week, are you really going to do me like this

﻿

Within 24 hours, #SaveChristophsDiamond became the most popular hashtag on all social media platforms. Fans all over the world were posting messages expressing their outrage towards Bruhmo Mint and starting petitions to return the diamond to Clean.

﻿

On his latest Instagram story, Clean announced that after finishing up the World Cup he is planning to file a lawsuit against Mint to rightfully get his diamond back.
